## FAQ Section (`#faq`)

- **Eyebrow:** FAQ
- **Heading:** Questions We Hear All The Time
- **Intro:** If you're wondering whether this is right for you, you're not alone. Here's what others ask.

**Q: Do I need to be unwell or injured to come here?**
No. A significant portion of our patients are healthy adults who want to stay that way, building and keeping the strength, muscle mass, and balance that make the difference in your 60s, 70s, and beyond. You don't need a condition. You just need a goal.

**Q: I'm in my 60s or 70s and I've never lifted a weight. Is it too late?**
Not even close. The research is unambiguous: adults in their 70s and 80s build muscle and strength in response to resistance training. The biological response is real at any age. The earlier you start, the more you keep, but starting late still produces meaningful change.

**Q: I have osteoporosis or arthritis, won't exercise make it worse?**
That's the most common fear we hear, and the most important to address. The right exercise actually strengthens bones, supports joints, and reduces pain over time. The wrong exercise (generic, unsupervised, poorly progressed) can flare things up. We'll make sure every exercise is appropriate for your specific condition and supervised by an Accredited Exercise Physiologist who works with these conditions regularly.

**Q: "I hate exercise", how is this different from a gym?**
Most of our patients said the same thing. We're a clinical practice, not a gym in the traditional sense. No loud music, no crowded classes, no judgement. It's just you and an expert who designs a program around your body and your goals. Most people find that when exercise doesn't hurt and actually makes them feel better, it stops feeling like a chore.

**Q: I'm rehabbing an injury, should I see a physio or an EP first?**
Generally, physio first for acute pain, then EP for the strength and loading work that prevents recurrence. We work closely with physiotherapists and many of our patients come to us straight after physio discharge for exactly this reason. If you're not sure, book an assessment and we'll be honest about whether you need physio first.

**Q: I'm busy. How much time do I need to commit?**
We know life is full. Sessions are scheduled at a frequency that works for you, whether that's weekly, fortnightly, or as a check-in every few weeks. Depending on your goals, we'll also include home- or gym-based programming you do between sessions. Consistency matters more than volume, and we'll find a rhythm that fits your schedule.

**Q: Do I need a referral? Can I claim rebates?**
You don't need a referral to book. However, if you have a chronic condition, you may be eligible for Medicare rebates under the Chronic Disease Management (CDM) plan with a referral from your GP. Most private health insurers also offer rebates for exercise physiology under extras cover. We can help you check your eligibility.

**Q: What happens in the first session?**
Your first session is a 60-minute initial assessment. We'll go through your health history, discuss what's bringing you in, identify your goals, and run baseline movement and strength tests. Then we'll put together a plan and talk through next steps. No workout required. Just a conversation and some simple assessments.
